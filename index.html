I am an assistant professor at the Federal Institute of the Southeast of Minas Gerais (IF Sudeste MG) and a Ph.D. student at the Department of Computer Science, Federal University of Minas Gerais (DCC-UFMG). At UFMG I work in the ASERG group under supervision of professor Marco Tulio Valente. I am broadly interested in developing use-inspired research in software engineering, with focus on techniques and methods for building and evolving complex software systems.


Previously, I completed my master degree in Computer Science under supervision of Carina Frota Alves at the Federal University of Pernambuco (CIn - UFPE) in 2016. During my master’s degree, I worked in an agreement project between CIn-UFPE and the Pernambuco Court of Accounts (TCE-PE). The purpose of the project was to create a framework for the evolution of TCE-PE Processes Office to support the improvement and automation of business processes of the Court.


Before this, I completed my degree in Internet Systems (2008) and Technical Course in Informatics (2004) by the Federal Institute of the Southeast of Minas Gerais (IF Sudeste MG).
